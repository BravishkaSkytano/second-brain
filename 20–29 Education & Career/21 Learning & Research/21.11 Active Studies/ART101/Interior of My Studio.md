## Description

- **Artist and Year**: Painted in 1855 by **Gustave Courbet**, this **Realist** painting is now housed at the **Musée d'Orsay** in Paris.
- **Location**: Created in **Ornans, France**, it reflects Courbet’s artistic and moral life over seven years.
- **Composition**:
	- **Left Side**: Depicts everyday people from all walks of life, including a **Jewish man** and an **Irishwoman** from Courbet’s travels.
	- **Center**: Shows Courbet painting a **landscape** (the **Loue River valley**) and a **nude model**, symbolizing **Academic art**.
	- **Right Side**: Features **Parisian elites** and figures influential in Courbet’s career, such as **Alfred Bruyas**, **Charles Baudelaire**, and **Pierre-Joseph Proudhon**.
- **Background**: The unfinished paintings on the studio wall represent Courbet’s earlier works, left incomplete due to time constraints.
- **Context**: The painting was not accepted for the **1855 Paris World Fair**, prompting Courbet to host his own exhibition, a precursor to the **Salon des Refusés**.
- **Themes**: Represents **society at its best, worst, and average**, reflecting Courbet’s **Realist** philosophy.
